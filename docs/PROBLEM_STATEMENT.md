# Problem Statement

## Title

Lack of Transparent and Reliable Trust Signals in Online Marketplaces

---

## Background

Online marketplaces rely heavily on star ratings and textual reviews to represent seller reliability. While these indicators are intended to guide consumer decisions, they often fail to provide a complete and trustworthy picture of a retailer’s actual performance.

Fake reviews, biased ratings, manipulated feedback, and lack of contextual understanding have significantly reduced consumer trust in existing systems.

---

## Core Problem

Consumers face difficulty in identifying trustworthy sellers due to:

- Over-reliance on star ratings without deeper behavioral insights
- Presence of fake or manipulated reviews
- No unified metric that evaluates overall seller reliability
- Lack of transparency in how trust signals are calculated
- Absence of real-time risk indicators during purchase decisions

As a result, buyers experience uncertainty, poor purchasing decisions, higher return rates, and reduced confidence in online shopping platforms.

---

## Impact on Consumer Experience

- Increased anxiety during purchase decisions
- Higher chances of fraud or misleading purchases
- Loss of trust in online marketplaces
- Reduced conversion rates and customer satisfaction

---

## Gap in Existing Solutions

Current marketplace trust mechanisms:

- Treat all reviews equally, regardless of authenticity
- Do not analyze seller behavior holistically
- Lack explainability and transparency
- Are not optimized for privacy-first, real-time AI execution

There is a clear need for an intelligent, transparent, and consumer-focused trust evaluation system.

---

## Problem to Solve

How can we design an AI-powered system that:

- Accurately evaluates retailer trustworthiness
- Detects and minimizes the impact of fake or biased reviews
- Provides explainable trust metrics to consumers
- Enhances consumer confidence without compromising privacy

## Proposed Direction

This problem motivates the development of a **Unified Retailer Trust Scoring System (URTSS)** that leverages AI and privacy-first local execution to transform how consumers evaluate seller trust in online marketplaces.
